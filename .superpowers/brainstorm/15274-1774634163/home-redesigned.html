What Changed & Why

Style: Modern Dark Cinema
Deep black base (#050506), animated ambient light blobs, no pure #000 (prevents OLED smear). Subtle glassmorphism on cards. The background feels alive — not static.
Palette: Mental Health Lavender
Primary #8B5CF6 (calming violet), secondary #C4B5FD (soft lavender), accent #059669 (wellness green), warm amber #F59E0B for streaks. Clinically calming — violet reduces anxiety in color psychology studies.
Typography: DM Sans + Playfair Display
Playfair for the greeting (warmth, personality, not robotic). DM Sans for everything else (clean, readable, premium). The serif heading makes this feel like a journal, not an app.
"Ready when you are"
Not "Good evening" — that's what every app says. This is a companion. It's patient. It doesn't judge when you opened it. It's just... ready.
"Try this" on 5-min pill
Adaptive nudge. If the user hasn't started in 3+ days, the app suggests 5 min to lower the barrier. After consistent use, this label disappears. The app learns.
Stats as ambient context
Three pills (streak, sessions, minutes) give a sense of progress without demanding attention. No graphs. No dashboards. Just three gentle numbers.
SVG icons, not emoji
Per UI/UX Pro guidelines: emoji renders inconsistently across platforms and looks unprofessional. Lucide-style SVG icons throughout.
Design Philosophy: Companion, Not Tool
This screen doesn't look like a Pomodoro timer. It looks like something that knows you. The serif heading, the patient language, the living background — it feels like opening a journal, not launching a utility.
