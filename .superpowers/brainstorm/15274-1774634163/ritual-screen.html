Ritual Design

Purpose: Mental Gear Shift
ADHD brains struggle with task-switching. A 12-second breathing pause creates a physical boundary between "scrolling mode" and "focus mode." It's not meditation — it's a gear shift.
Always Skippable
Skip button top-right. "Tap anywhere to skip" at bottom. Two escape routes. ADHD users viscerally hate forced waits — a mandatory ritual would get the app uninstalled. Making it optional makes it feel like a gift, not a gate.
The Breathing Pattern: 4-4-4
4 seconds in → 4 seconds hold → 4 seconds out. Three cycles = 36 seconds max. But users can skip after the first cycle (12s). The dots show progress through cycles.
Visual Language
Expanding/contracting circle with concentric ripple rings. Ambient blob intensifies during inhale, fades during exhale. The entire screen breathes with you. No numbers, no countdown — pure visual rhythm.
Micro-copy: Body Cues
Below the breathing phase, a body-awareness prompt rotates: "Let your shoulders drop," "Unclench your jaw," "Feet flat on the floor." Grounds the user in their body — a technique from ADHD therapy.
Transition Out
After the last exhale (or skip), the breathing circle morphs into the timer ring via a shared-element transition. The violet glow carries forward. Spatial continuity — per UI/UX Pro animation guidelines.
